Hot Chocolate

Prep and Cook Time

Prep Time Cook Time Total Time
5 mins 10 mins 15 mins

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole milk
    • Add 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon, 1/16 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper, and the tiniest pinch of salt (less than 1/16 teaspoon) to the milk before heating to make it spiced and reminiscent of a Mexican hot chocolate.
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 4 oz bittersweet chocolate
    • You can use other types of chocolate than the bittersweet, such as milk, semisweet, white, or even Ruby. You may want to cut back on the sugar in those cases since they are sweeter.
  • Splash of vanilla extract (optiona)
    • Instead of a splash of vanilla extract, swap in some peppermint extract to make it a little minty!
  • Whipped cream (optional)
  • Marshmallows (optional)

Instructions

  1. Combine milk and sugar in a small saucepan and place on the stovetop over medium heat.
  2. While the milk is heating place the chopped chocolate in the microwave and heat for 30 seconds. Take the chocolate out and stir it. Return to microwave and heat for another thirty seconds, remove and stir.
  3. Continue to heat and stir the chocolate in 30 second intervals until it has *almost* melted. Always err on the side of undermelting, so you don't risk overheating it and causing separation and grainyness.
  4. When the milk reaches the scalding point (180 degrees F, with bubbles on the side), turn off the heat and add the melted chocolate, whisking to combine. Add the vanilla extract now, if desired. Top with whipped cream or marshmallows if using, and enjoy!
